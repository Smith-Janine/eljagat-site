Case Study 001 — Increasing Engagement for MLL & IEP Students

Problem
An MLL student was disengaging due to language barriers, and an IEP student struggled to sustain focus during independent tasks. Both students showed patterns of starting work but not maintaining engagement.

System Implemented
- 3-task scaffolded entry system
- Targeted language support (Spanish keywords)
- Student task tracker with check-ins
- Structured task sequencing

Execution
The system was implemented during Do Now and guided practice. Students were given clearly defined tasks with reduced entry barriers. Engagement was monitored through check-ins and task completion tracking.

Result
The MLL student showed immediate engagement and successfully completed tasks but lost stamina over time. The IEP student demonstrated strong initial engagement when structure was present but disengaged when that structure was no longer actively reinforced.

Adjustment
Next iteration will include:
- Time-bound micro-tasks (2-minute work blocks)
- Mid-task resets to sustain focus
- Task-based completion tracking instead of full assignment completion

Case Study 002 — Building Personal Regulation Through Structured Systems

Problem
Inconsistent routines, scattered focus, and difficulty sustaining attention across tasks. This resulted in low execution consistency and reactive decision-making, particularly around time management and eating habits.

System Implemented
- Fixed morning anchor routine
- Single-task work block system (45-minute execution)
- Elimination of multitasking during meals
- Daily performance tracking (tasks planned vs completed)

Execution
Systems are implemented daily with a focus on consistency over intensity. Each day includes a defined starting routine, one focused work block, and tracked task completion.

Result
Early-stage implementation shows increased awareness of behavioral patterns, improved task initiation, and clearer identification of breakdown points in attention and energy regulation.

Adjustment
Next iteration will focus on:
- Maintaining consistency across multiple days
- Reducing cognitive overload by limiting daily priorities
- Strengthening routine adherence without increasing complexity
